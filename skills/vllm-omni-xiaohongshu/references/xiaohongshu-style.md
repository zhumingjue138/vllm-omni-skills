# Xiaohongshu Style

Use this file when shaping the final post for Xiaohongshu.

## Audience

Default audience:

- Chinese-speaking AI developers
- OSS maintainers watching inference stacks
- model builders evaluating multimodal serving options
- technically curious creators who want practical takeaways

Write for informed readers, not complete beginners.

## Post Shape

Aim for:

- one main claim
- a fast hook in the first line
- short paragraphs or list-like blocks
- one proof block with numbers, commands, or a concrete example
- a closing line that tells readers who should care or what to try next

## Title Patterns

Use titles that expose the outcome, not vague excitement.

Good patterns:

- "vLLM-Omni now supports <model>: what changed and who it helps"
- "We cut <thing> from <before> to <after> in vLLM-Omni"
- "This vLLM-Omni update makes <workflow> much easier"
- "A painful <failure mode> in vLLM-Omni is finally fixed"

Avoid:

- empty hype
- titles that hide the subject until the end
- unsupported superlatives

## Tone

- clear
- technically literate
- concrete
- calm rather than salesy

Prefer:

- naming the exact model, endpoint, config, or command
- short sentences
- one sentence per idea

Avoid:

- jargon stacking
- exaggerated emotional language
- five different claims in one post

## Hashtags

Use 5-8 focused tags. Mix:

- project tag: `#vLLMOmni`
- modality or model tag: `#Multimodal`, `#TTS`, `#ImageGeneration`
- problem tag: `#Inference`, `#ModelServing`, `#OpenSourceAI`
- scenario tag when relevant: `#PerformanceOptimization`, `#Deployment`

Do not pad with generic trending tags that do not match the content.

## Cover Text

Keep cover text short and outcome-driven. Good cover text usually states:

- what changed
- what improved
- who should care

Examples of structure:

- "<model> support in vLLM-Omni"
- "<metric> improved"
- "A simpler way to serve <thing>"

## Final Check

Before final output, verify:

- the first line is understandable without extra repo context
- the strongest claim has proof
- technical nouns are spelled correctly
- the post does not imply release status beyond the source
